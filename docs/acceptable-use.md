# PartyPad acceptable use summary

Status: pre-pilot draft.

PartyPad is for consensual multiplayer controller sessions. Do not use it to:

- access a laptop or session without the host's permission;
- share a controller QR/link beyond the people invited to that game;
- automate device codes, sessions, TURN allocations, or controller traffic;
- evade quotas, rate limits, revocation, or a service capacity pause;
- probe, disrupt, or impose unreasonable cost on PartyPad, Cloudflare, a school,
  venue, carrier, or another person's network;
- transmit unlawful, abusive, or malicious content.

The operator may reject new sessions, revoke abusive devices, or temporarily
limit enrollment to a separately defined pilot cohort while capacity is being
measured. A cohort is a rollout control, not a permanent university or email
domain eligibility rule. Existing game sessions should not be terminated by a
routine capacity pause.

Controller links are bearer credentials. Hosts should stop a session after play
and avoid posting its QR code publicly. Testers should report security problems
privately as described in `SECURITY.md`.
